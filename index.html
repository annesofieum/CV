Motivation

Why I'm passionate about joining Dragon Shield as a Product Manager and how my gaming background aligns with the company's mission.

I've worked in fashion for years, which taught me a lot about aesthetics and understanding what people want. But let's be honest — trying to explain why I spend my evenings grinding for a new mount in WoW to people who think gaming is "just for kids" gets old fast.

I'm the person who will drag you into a 30-minute debate about Harry Potter vs. Star Wars vs. Lord of The Rings, and I'll probably win because I've thought about it way too much. I've been gaming since I was a preteen, and those late-night WoW raids taught me more about teamwork and problem-solving than most corporate workshops ever will.

Here's what I learned from working with 50+ international brands: most people know when something feels wrong with a product, but they struggle to explain what exactly bothers them. Gamers are different. We'll write you a detailed analysis on why this particular shade of blue doesn't work for a fantasy theme. We're incredibly specific about aesthetics and understanding what people want, and we're not embarrassed about caring that much.

Dragon Shield caught my attention because of your approach to work culture. You get that passion isn't unprofessional — it's an asset. When I see job postings that mention "sword fights at summer parties," I know you understand that the best work happens when people can be genuinely excited about what they're building.

I'm a problem-solver by nature. Give me a challenge and I'll dig into it until I understand not just what's happening, but why it matters to the people involved. I want to work somewhere I can be nerdy about products with people who get it. Dragon Shield feels like that place.
